Homemade Chicken Tikka Masala Recipe

Chicken Tikka Masala is a flavorful Indian cuisine that originated from an Indian restaurant in Scotland that was owned by a British Bangladeshi community.

Tikka stands for bits or pieces of chicken marinated in spices and yogurt and then grilled to perfection. These tikkas are then cooked in a medium to spicy rich creamy tomato gravy. Vegeterians can substitue chicken for cottage cheese or paneer. This gravy is garnished with fresh cilantro leaves and is served with white rice or naan;an oven-baked flatbread. You can find all the ingredients for this recipe from any indian grocery store.

Cooking this gravy is not quick and easy but the end result is delicious in every bite and worth the effort. Enjoy your meal with this lip-smacking gravy.

INGREDIENTS

Servings: 5-6

For Chicken Marinade

  • 3 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 lemon
  • 125 grams yogurt
  • 6 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 teaspoons cumin
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • Bamboo or wooden skewers

For gravy

  • 3 tablespoons oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger, minced
  • 8 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons cumin
  • 2 teaspoons turmeric
  • 2 teaspoons ground coriander
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 2 teaspoons chili powder
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon tomato puree
  • 800 grams tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • 300 milliliters water
  • 250 milliliters cream

For Serving

  • Chopped cilantro leaves,to garnish
  • Rice and naan bread,to serve

PREPARATION

  1. Preheat oven to 500°F/260°C.
  2. Slice the chicken into bite-sized chunks, then combine the chicken with the rest of the chicken marinade ingredients in a bowl. Mix until evenly coated, then refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  3. Skewer the marinated chicken pieces on bamboo skewers, then place them over a baking tray lined with parchment paper, making sure there is space underneath the chicken to help distribute the heat more evenly.
  4. Bake for about 15 minutes until slightly dark brown on the edges.
  5. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat, then sauté the onions, ginger, and garlic until tender but not browned.
  6. Fry the spices for about 30 seconds to release their aromatics and flavor, stirring constantly, then stir in the tomato puree.
  7. Add the tomato sauce and water, then bring to a boil and cook for about 5 minutes.
  8. Pour in the cream and mix in the chicken, cooking for another 1-2 minutes.
  9. Serve with rice and naan bread!
